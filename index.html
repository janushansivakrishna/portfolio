Rankine Cycle Plant Design

Oct 2025 – Dec 2025 • Toronto Metropolitan University

Designed an 80 MWe / 25 MWth natural gas Rankine cycle power plant to provide sustainable power and district heating for remote Canadian communities. Focused on maximizing thermal efficiency and economic viability through advanced cycle optimization.

  • Engineered a four-stage turbine setup with reheat and regenerative cycles; compared three iterations.
  • Integrated three feedwater heaters (two closed, one open) and a 0.08 bar condenser to recover 125°C waste heat for district heating.
  • Validated with MATLAB simulations using the XSteam library; performed methane cost and moisture-removal analyses.

Results: Mass flow 78.31 kg/s • Thermal power 135.39 MW • Electrical output 80.00 MW • Efficiency 45.47%
